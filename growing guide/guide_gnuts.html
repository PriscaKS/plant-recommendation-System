Growing Guide for Gnuts

Instructions

This is the growing guide for Gnuts.

Seasonal Recommendations

  • Spring: Start planting Gnuts after the last frost date, usually in late spring. Ensure the soil temperature has warmed to at least 65Â°F for optimal germination.
  • Summer: Maintain consistent watering throughout the summer months, especially during dry spells. Mulch around the plants to retain soil moisture and suppress weeds.
  • Fall: Harvest Gnuts in the fall when the plants begin to die back. Remove any remaining pods from the soil to prevent disease overwintering.
  • Winter: Gnuts are not cold-hardy and will die back in freezing temperatures. If grown as a perennial, provide protection from frost with row covers or bring containers indoors.
